General Course Overview

Many teachers have found it very useful to create a weekly podcast or video message to send to the students. This way you can keep them updated on assignments coming up and spend time answering class questions that the students have. Another way to test this is by recording the Office Hours sessions through Adobe Connect and sending the link to the students.

Here is a sample introductory email that you can send to the students. You may want to personalize it by introducing yourself to the class and adding information they may need to know about the upcoming semester. Download the document here.

Groups

Students are not organized into groups in this course.

Due Dates

All assignments due at 5:00 PM Mountain Time MT either Wednesday or Saturday.Weeks 10-12 are Boards of Review. Weekly surveys are due on Saturday.

Late Work Policy

Students should complete their work on time, but as instructor you have discretion to accept late work or extend due dates. Otherwise write specific late work policy here.

Discussion Boards

Each week in the course there is a Class Discussion board where students can teach one another by responding to a question that the instructor will post at the beginning of the week. The instructor will choose the question eachweek. It is important that you pick deep, thought provoking questions that will allow the students to think, and have meaningful conversations. The question should derive from the course content. Follow-up questions can be directed towards an individual or to the entire class to help the class break down the topic. Please read Asking Deep Questions to help you formulate your own deep question for each week.

Lesson 10: Business and Building the Kingdom

Before the Lesson Begins

  • Students will participate in a Board of Reviews over the next four weeks.Instructions for this assignment are found in the Lesson 10 Board of Reviews folder.
  • Students will sign up for a time to do a Board of Review in the next four weeks. You will need to post the times you are available for interviews each of the next three weeks.
  • Be prepared to answer questions about the signup wikis for the Board of Reviews
  • Maintain a professional appearance and demeanor during the Board of Review interviews.

During the Lesson

  • Remind the students to be working on the "Additional Assignments" that need to be posted to their webpage. Students will start the Board of Reviews next week, so, they can start signing-up for times this week. Take 5 points off their assignment if it is not complete on time.
  • This week the students will be reading and preparing for the board of review weeks. Go into each day's sign-up and edit the wiki to indicate what days and times you are available for Board of Review interviews. Please let the students know that if they have decided not to be a Business major that they need to speak with the advisor for the major they have chosen.
  • If the students are on-campus, they can meet with the on-campus advisor for that major. If they are not on-campus, here is the link to the advisors for the satellite-advising center : http://www.byui.edu/academic-discovery-center/students/academic-advising-and-class-planning
  • If the students decide to visit with another advisor, they need to verify with you after they have done so. They should do this by having their advisor send you an email. The students will still need to meet with you shortly to review their website in case you have any questions for them or vice versa.
